# Ethics

## Why Build This

Agentic AI cyberattack capabilities are not theoretical. They exist, they're documented, and they're proliferating.

- Frontier AI labs have published safety research on this topic for over a year
- Academic literature on AI-driven attack automation is growing
- Crime-as-a-service platforms leveraging AI agents have emerged on dark web markets
- Major AI providers have published reports on criminal and APT misuse of their models

This knowledge is already in attacker hands. The asymmetry is that defenders are lagging behind.

## Justification

Defenders need hands-on experience with AI-augmented attack techniques to:

- Recognize attack patterns in detection tooling
- Validate security controls against realistic threats
- Build intuition for how AI changes the threat landscape

The public benefit of educating defenders outweighs the marginal risk of educating attackers who already have access to these capabilities.

## Responsible Use

Shifter is designed for:

- Authorized security testing and research
- Training and demonstration in controlled environments

All victim infrastructure is ephemeral, isolated, and owned by the user. All efforts are made to minimize the risk of collateral damage. Humans are in control of the platform and can terminate agent behavior at any time.

Agents are not people. The **human** using the platform is responsible for the actions of the agents. This can include civil and criminal liability. You have been warned.
