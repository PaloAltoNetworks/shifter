### Discovery Phase

**Questions to Ask**:
- "How do you currently test your security tools?"
- "What's your process for validating detection capabilities?"
- "Have you seen AI-driven attacks in your environment?"
- "How confident is your team in your current XDR solution?"

**Listen For**:
- Concerns about false positives/negatives

### Value Positioning

**The Setup**:
*"I want to show you something different from our typical demo. Instead of slides or recorded videos, we can run a live AI-driven attack against real XDR-protected infrastructure—and you'll see actual detections in your own tenant. Takes about 30 minutes. Sound interesting?"*

**The Hook**:
- **Live**: Not recorded, not scripted
- **Your environment**: Uses your XDR tenant
- **AI-powered**: Autonomous attacks (what adversaries use)
- **Immediate**: Can run it this week

**The Business Value**:
- Prove XDR capabilities before purchase
- Validate detection accuracy
- See ROI of XDR investment
- Educate team on modern threats

## Demo Coordination (Working with Your SE)

### Your Role
- **Qualify the opportunity**: Ensure it's a good fit
- **Set up the meeting**: 45-60 minute slot
- **Manage attendees**: Right people in the room
- **Drive business discussion**: Connect demo to their needs
- **Handle next steps**: POC proposal, contracting, etc.

### SE's Role
- **Execute demo**: Platform operation, attack scenarios
- **Technical Q&A**: Answer detailed questions
- **Show detections**: Navigate XDR console
- **Validate capabilities**: Prove technical value

### Meeting Structure (45-60 minutes)

**Minutes 0-5**: Introductions & Context (You lead)
- Set agenda
- Understand customer's current state
- Explain what they'll see

**Minutes 5-35**: Live Demo (SE leads)
- Launch range
- Run attack scenario
- Show XDR detections
- Answer technical questions

**Minutes 35-45**: Business Discussion (You lead)
- Connect to customer's needs
- Address concerns or objections
- Propose next steps
- Define success criteria

**Minutes 45-60**: Q&A and Close (Both)
- Final questions
- Commit to action items
- Schedule follow-up

## Objection Handling

### "We're already happy with our current XDR"
**Response**: "That's great! Let me show you what makes PANW different. 30 minutes to see live AI attacks against your current solution—you might be surprised what you're missing."

**Follow-up**: Position as competitive validation, show gaps in their current tool.

---

### "We don't have time for another demo"
**Response**: "I understand. This is different—30 minutes, live in your environment, shows exactly what you care about. Most customers say it's the most valuable demo they've seen."

**Follow-up**: Offer specific time slot, emphasize efficiency.

---

### "Sounds too technical for our team"
**Response**: "We tailor it to your audience. For executives, we focus on business risk and AI threats. For technical teams, we dive into detection details. What matters most to you?"

**Follow-up**: Adjust demo scenario to their level.

---

### "How much does this cost?"
**Response**: "Shifter is a PANW demo tool—there's no separate cost. The value is in validating your XDR investment before you commit."

**Follow-up**: Redirect to XDR/XSIAM pricing discussion.

---

### "Is this safe/compliant?"
**Response**: "Completely isolated environment, no internet egress, your data stays in your tenant. It's designed for secure testing."

**Follow-up**: Share security documentation if needed.
